A. Behavioral Neurologists, M.D.'s

Behavioral neurologists often diagnose dementia, which includes Alzheimer's disease
Behavioral neurologists may offer the following treatments:

1. Cognitive behavioral therapy
(CBT): A language-based therapy that may be difficult for people with dementia who have language difficulties.
2. Exercise:
Regular exercise, such as walking, may help reduce aggression.
3. Touch therapies:
Hand massages can help reduce aggressive behaviors, while expressive physical touch can help lower anxiety and reduce dysfunctional behaviors.
4. Cognitive activities:
Simple cognitive activities may help.

B. Psychologists Treaments for Alzheimers

1. Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT)
A type of psychotherapy that can help with anxiety and depression. CBT is based on the idea that how we think affects how we feel and behave. CBT for anxiety or depression is best for people in the earlier stages of dementia.

2. Cognitive stimulation therapy (CST) A group therapy that involves activities and exercises to help with memory and problem-solving. CST is for patients with mild to moderate dementia.

3. Psychodynamic interpersonal therapy
A type of individual therapy led by a clinical psychologist.

4. Talk therapy
Can include sessions with a mental health counselor or support group to help with the emotional challenges of Alzheimer's.
